# OBAG: migration and culture

Post link: https://www.facebook.com/groups/openborders/posts/25670907412579304

## Post content

A lot has been written about how migration can change culture, but I think migration plays a really important role in helping geographical areas maintain their distinctive culture! That's because of the self-selection of people migrating to and from the place.

Major metropolitan areas with distinctive cultures, whether it's the New York City area, the San Francisco Bay Area, or the Los Angeles area in the United States, or the metropolitan areas of Mumbai, Bengaluru, or Delhi in India, would have a lot more trouble maintaining their distinctive culture if there wasn't a much larger population of people who could migrate to the area.

For instance, the Los Angeles metropolitan area, which is home to a lot of the movie and influencer industry in the United States, wouldn't be able to do this if it were limited to the pool of people who were born in the area! A large number of people from all around the United States, and in many cases, from elsewhere in the world, move to LA and help maintain its relevance in those respects.

On the flip side, quiet laid-back towns are better able to maintain the laid-back culture partly because those who find the laid-back culture stifling are free to move to places that are better fits for their ambition!

I think the "migration will radically change culture" argument often carries an assumption that culture is transmitted from parent to child, and persists generationally, and while there is some transmission (with both nature and nurture playing some role) there's a lot of "regression to the mean" -- children can want very different things than their parents did, and their wants can also evolve over time as their beliefs and life situations change. Migration allows people to make their geographical home match their current identity, and in turn helps geographical locations maintain their own distinct culture.
